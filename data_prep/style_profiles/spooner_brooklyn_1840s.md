# Spooner / Teale — early Brooklyn City Directory, 1830s–1840s

**Representative volume:** `ia/micro_IABROOKLYN_0026` ≡ `ia/brooklyncitydire1848teal` (the **same**
1848-49 volume — microfilm + regular-IA scans) — *Brooklyn City Directory, and Annual Advertiser
[for 1848-49]*, compiled by **Thomas P. Teale**, published by **E. B. Spooner** (Printer, Franklin
Buildings, cor. Fulton and Orange sts., Brooklyn). Visually sampled 2026-06-19 (session 4).

## Structure
- Title page (microfilm leaf ~2): *"BROOKLYN CITY DIRECTORY, AND ANNUAL ADVERTISER … COMPILED BY
  THOMAS P. TEALE. PUBLISHED BY E. B. SPOONER … 1848."* Preface "TO THE REGISTER" (signed Teale,
  Brooklyn June 26 1848) precedes the alphabetical body.
- `column_count` = **1** (single full-width column) — confirmed on the 1848-49 listing (leaf 135,
  printed p.167, MAC section). This anchors the **whole pre-Lain Brooklyn run (1830–1849) as col=1**
  and brackets the Brooklyn 1→2-column transition to **1855–1858** (Hearne still col-1 in 1855 ↔
  Lain already col-2 in 1858).
- Microfilm (`micro_IABROOKLYN_*`) scans are single-page single-column (NOT spreads), just dark.

## Entry format
`Surname Firstname, occupation [Address with street-relations] [N Y] [h HomeAddr]` — the same
**Brooklyn street-relation grammar** as Hearne 1850s (`n` near, `c` corner, `r` rear; `N Y` = works
in Manhattan; widow spelled out). See [hearne_brooklyn_1850s.md].

Verbatim (1848-49, leaf 135 / printed p.167, MAC–NEA):
- `Millan John, candy store Fulton c Fleet`
- `Mullen Mary, widow 5 Halls alley r Furman st`
- `Murray Joseph, shipping merchant N Y h 79 Pierrepont`
- `Nally John, foundry man Myrtle n Clinton`

## Cohort & publishers (1830–1849, ~23 rows; col=1 backfilled session 4)
The early Brooklyn run is `micro_IABROOKLYN_0007–0027` (1830–1849) plus regular-IA
`brooklynalphabet1843unse` (1843) and `brooklyncitydire1848teal` (1848). All **col=1**.
**Publishers vary and are mostly unverified** — Spooner confirmed only for 1848-49 (Teale/Spooner);
the 1856-57 "Consolidated" is Hope & Henderson (already in CSV); Lain takes over by 1858.
`publisher` left blank for the unverified early years (notes tag them "Spooner-era").

**Duplicates noted:** `micro_IABROOKLYN_0026` ≡ `brooklyncitydire1848teal` (1848-49);
`brooklynalphabet1843unse` (1843) likely ≡ `micro_IABROOKLYN_0019` (1843). Dedupe at sample time.

## Genre
Residential persons directory — Brooklyn (KEEP shape), pre-Lain. Compiler Teale / publisher Spooner.

# Hodge, Allen & Campbell — The New-York Directory and Register, 1790

**Representative volume:** `nypl/614e2e50-81ad-0134-e971-00505686a51c` — *The New-York Directory, and
Register, for the Year 1790. Illustrated with an accurate and elegant Plan of the City of New-York…
Printed by Hodge, Allen, and Campbell.* Visually sampled 2026-06-29 (front matter + listing-start
canvas 12 / printed p.5).

**Scope:** Residential + business persons directory, **Manhattan** (KEEP shape). `column_count = 1`.
One of the earliest NYC directories (4 years after Franks 1786). Structure: title page (canvas 8) →
**Register** section (contents of occupations, banks, customs officers, congress, societies — *not* a
training target, canvases 9–11, printed pp.3–4) → **persons listing begins at printed p.5 (canvas 12)**.
`page_offset` = **+7** (canvas 12 = printed p.5; canvas 13 = printed p.6).

## Key page

**No dedicated "Abbreviations" legend page** (1790-era volumes predate the formal key page). The listing
opens directly under the head "THE NEW-YORK DIRECTORY, &c." with the letter "A". Marks are sparse and
mostly spelled out.

## Entry format

`Surname, Given, occupation, [corner of / near] Street`

Verbatim samples (canvas 12, listing start, printed p.5):
```
ABOUT, Adam, grocer, corner of Smith and Little Queen-Street
```

- Comma after the **surname** (the oldest punctuation style — same as Franks 1786 and Longworth 1797;
  the comma migrates to after the given name by the 1820s).
- Street relations are **spelled out** ("corner of", "near", "between") rather than abbreviated to
  `c.`/`n.` — the abbreviated grammar arrives later.
- Single address per entry (work-and-home not yet separated); firms follow the same template.

## Abbreviations / localities

Mostly spelled out ("corner of", "near"); `do.` = ditto for a repeated street. All verbatim in gold.

## Genre

Residential persons directory bundled with a **Register** (almanac-like reference section) — the same
shape as Franks 1786 and the early Longworth run. The Register precedes the alphabetical listing and is
excluded from the persons-extraction target. See `franks_newyork_1786.md`, `longworth_manhattan_1830s.md`.

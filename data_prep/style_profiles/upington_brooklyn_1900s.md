# Upington — General Directory of the Borough of Brooklyn, 1900s

**Representative volume:** `ia/brooklynnewyorkc1906geor` — *Upington's General Directory of the
Borough of Brooklyn, City of New York, Volume LXXXIII, for the year 1906* (George Upington,
Publisher; Office 317 Washington St., Brooklyn). Visually sampled 2026-06-19 (session 4).

## Structure (jp2 leaf indices, 0-based)
- Leaf 3: IA digitization page. Leaf 4: verso of title (visible as reversed bleed-through).
  **Leaf 6 = title page** (*"UPINGTON'S GENERAL DIRECTORY OF THE BOROUGH OF BROOKLYN … VOLUME LXXXIII
  … FOR THE YEAR 1906 … GEORGE UPINGTON, Publisher … PRICE $8.00"*). The `geor` in the IA id =
  **GEOR**ge Upington.
- `column_count` = **2** — two text columns separated by a rule, with **vertical ad strips in both
  outer margins** (e.g. "Ernestus Gulick Co. Estate Agents", "F.W. Flagler Carriages", and the
  publisher's own "Upington's Directory Office, 317 Washington St., Brooklyn"). Same marginal-ad
  layout family as Lain.
- `page_offset`: not measured (ad-block length varies). Listing sampled at leaf 367 (CANNELL–CANTLER)
  and leaf 855.

## Entry format — CORRECTED 2026-06-20 (hi-res read of leaves 367 & 855)
`Surname Given occupation [employer/work addr] h HomeAddr` — **NO commas between fields**
(space-delimited), unlike Lain (which is comma-delimited). Three corrections to the session-4 guess:
1. **No commas.** `Cannell Jos clk h 379 Gold` · `Canning And'w shoes 960 Myrtle av` ·
   `Hugh engineer h 448 Carlton av`.
2. **Ditto = a leading `"`** that repeats the bold-caps headword surname (so the full surname is
   **not** repeated each line): `Greenberg Jacob tailor 559 Marcy av` → `" Louis hfurng 82 Belmont av`
   → `" Nathan tailor h 752 H'pkson`.
3. **Given names abbreviated** (Jos, Edw'd, And'w, H'y, Dan'l, Cath). Prominent headword surnames
   in bold caps.
